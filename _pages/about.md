I am a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science at Missouri University of Science and Technology, working in the area of **AI, computer vision, and visual localization** under the guidance of Dr. Sanjay Madria.

My research focuses on developing **robust, passive localization systems for GPS-denied environments**, with applications in autonomous navigation, robotics, and defense scenarios. ## Current Work

### LanBLoc: Landmark-Based Visual Localization

I am developing **LanBLoc**, a passive visual localization framework that replaces traditional radio-based anchors with pre-mapped visual landmarks.

Key components include:

- **Landmark detection** using deep learning models (YOLO-based architectures)
- **Stereo vision** for distance estimation
- **Geometric localization** via trilateration using detected landmarks
- **Robust evaluation** against state-of-the-art SLAM and VO systems

This approach enables accurate localization in environments where GPS signals are unavailable, unreliable, or intentionally denied.
